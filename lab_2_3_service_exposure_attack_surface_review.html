1. Lab Purpose

The purpose of this lab is to help students understand service exposure and attack surface. Students will observe which services are running locally on Ubuntu-Target, which services are visible remotely from Kali-Lab, and how disabling an unnecessary service reduces exposure.

Main goal: Students should learn that every unnecessary listening service increases attack surface, and that defenders should compare local system evidence with remote network evidence.

2. Learning Objectives

  1. Define service exposure and attack surface.
  2. Identify running services locally using systemctl.
  3. Identify listening ports locally using ss.
  4. Use Nmap from Kali-Lab to observe remotely visible ports.
  5. Start a temporary test service on Ubuntu-Target to simulate increased exposure.
  6. Scan before and after adding the temporary test service.
  7. Disable the unnecessary service and verify exposure reduction.
  8. Compare local evidence, remote scan evidence, and firewall state.
  9. Create a before/after attack surface review report.
  10. Explain OS-specific differences across Windows, Intel Mac, Apple Silicon Mac, Kali, and Ubuntu environments.

3. Lab Scenario

You are reviewing a small lab server before it is used in later security exercises. The server may have services that are necessary, unnecessary, or accidentally exposed. Your task is to document current exposure, simulate how adding a service changes attack surface, then remove that unnecessary exposure and verify the result.

6. Safety Rules

  1. Scan only your assigned Ubuntu-Target VM and authorized lab subnet.
  2. Do not scan public websites, school production networks, home routers, or classmates’ machines.
  3. Do not use exploit scripts, brute force, denial-of-service, or vulnerability exploitation tools.
  4. Use the temporary HTTP service only on Ubuntu-Target inside the isolated lab network.
  5. Stop the temporary HTTP service before finishing the lab.
  6. Do not disable critical services unless the lab explicitly instructs you to do so.
  7. Keep the virtual network host-only/private unless the instructor authorizes otherwise.
Important: This lab is about exposure review and defensive reduction. It is not an attack lab.

7. Key Concepts

Service Exposure

Service exposure refers to services that are reachable over the network. A service may be running locally but not reachable remotely, or it may be visible to other hosts.

Attack Surface

Attack surface is the set of points where an attacker could interact with a system. Open ports, exposed services, weak configurations, unnecessary accounts, and outdated software can all increase attack surface.

15. Troubleshooting Guide

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Kali cannot ping UbuntuWrong IP or VMs are on different networksVerify ip -brief addr and host-only/private networking.
Port 8080 does not appear openTemporary service is not running or wrong IP usedVerify python3 -m http.server is running and check ss.
Curl cannot connect to port 8080Wrong target IP, service stopped, or firewall blockingCheck Ubuntu IP, service status, and ufw.

Nmap shows different results than ssFirewall, local-only binding, or scan timing differenceCompare listener address, firewall status, and scan output.
scp failsWrong username, IP, path, or SSH service issueVerify SSH access and folder paths.
ufw not installedFirewall package unavailableDocument that UFW evidence was unavailable; do not install unless permitted.
