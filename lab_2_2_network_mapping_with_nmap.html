1. Lab Purpose

The purpose of this lab is to introduce safe network mapping using Nmap. Students will identify hosts, ports, and services only within their assigned virtual lab environment. Students will compare remote scan results with local evidence from Ubuntu-Target to understand what Nmap can and cannot see.

Main goal: Students should learn that network mapping is an authorized discovery activity used to understand the attack surface of systems they are responsible for protecting.

2. Learning Objectives

  1. Explain the purpose of network mapping in security assessment and defense.
  2. Identify the correct isolated lab subnet before scanning.
  3. Use Nmap host discovery to identify authorized lab hosts.
  4. Run safe TCP port scans against Ubuntu-Target.
  5. Identify open, closed, and filtered port states.
  6. Use service-version detection to identify exposed services.
  7. Compare Nmap results with local ss and systemctl evidence on Ubuntu.
  8. Save Nmap results using normal, grepable, and XML output formats.
  9. Create a network map and service exposure summary.
  10. Explain OS-specific differences across Windows, Intel Mac, Apple Silicon Mac, Kali, and Ubuntu environments.

3. Lab Scenario

You are a security analyst documenting a small isolated lab network. Before firewall, vulnerability scanning, IDS, or incident response labs, you must identify which lab hosts are online and what services are reachable over the network. Your scan must be limited to your own lab VMs.

6. Safety Rules

  1. Scan only the lab IP addresses assigned to you.
  2. Do not scan public websites, school networks, home routers, or classmates’ machines.
  3. Do not use aggressive scan options against systems outside your lab.
  4. Do not use vulnerability exploitation scripts in this lab.
  5. Do not use denial-of-service, brute-force, or stress-test options.
  6. Keep the lab network host-only/private unless the instructor explicitly authorizes otherwise.
  7. Document the scan scope before scanning.
Important: Nmap is a legitimate security tool, but scanning systems without permission can violate policy or law. This lab is limited to your isolated virtual lab only.

7. Key Concepts

Network Mapping

Network mapping is the process of identifying hosts, IP addresses, open ports, and reachable services. Defenders use mapping to understand what is exposed and to reduce unnecessary attack surface.

Common Nmap Scan Types in This Lab

Nmap OptionMeaningUse in This Lab
-snHost discovery without port scanningFind online lab hosts
-sTTCP connect scanSafe scan option when not using raw packets
-sSTCP SYN scanRequires sudo; common scan type on Kali
-sVService version detectionIdentify service names and versions
-OOS detectionOptional; may be inaccurate in small VM labs
-pSpecify portsLimit scans to selected ports
-oASave output in multiple formatsSave normal, grepable, and XML evidence

Port States

StateMeaning
OpenA service is accepting connections on the port.
ClosedThe host is reachable, but no service is listening on the port.
FilteredNmap cannot determine whether the port is open because traffic is blocked or filtered.

Part 6 — Host Discovery Scan

On Kali-Lab, identify your lab subnet from ip route. Then run a host discovery scan only against your authorized subnet.

nmap -sn YOUR-LAB-SUBNET -oA ~/Lab2-2-Nmap/host-discovery/lab-subnet-discovery

Example only:

nmap -sn 192.168.56.0/24 -oA ~/Lab2-2-Nmap/host-discovery/lab-subnet-discovery

Review the normal output:

cat ~/Lab2-2-Nmap/host-discovery/lab-subnet-discovery.nmap

15. Troubleshooting Guide

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Kali cannot ping UbuntuWrong IP address or VMs are on different networksVerify ip -brief addr and host-only/private networking.
Nmap finds no hostsWrong subnet or target powered offConfirm target IP with hostname -I on Ubuntu and scan only that subnet.
Nmap says host seems downICMP may be blocked or wrong IP usedVerify IP address and try scanning the specific authorized IP.
No open ports are foundNo services are listening or firewall blocks accessCheck Ubuntu with sudo ss -tulpen and sudo ufw status verbose.
-sS scan failsSYN scan requires elevated privilegesUse sudo nmap -sS or use nmap -sT.
OS detection failsNot enough open/closed ports or VM fingerprint ambiguityDocument that OS detection was inconclusive.
scp failsWrong username, IP, path, or SSH service issueVerify SSH login and the evidence folder path.
